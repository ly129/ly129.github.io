About me

My research experience and expertise encompass high-dimensional statistical learning, federated learning, missing data methods, integrative analysis for heterogeneous data, cancer epidemiology, cardio-oncology and pharmacoepidemiology.

I am passionate about addressing concrete and meaningful clinical research questions using both classical and novel statistical and machine learning methods, leveraging data from electronic health records and clinical trials.

I am particularly interested in clinical trials. I have rich experience in statistical analysis planning, analysis and reporting for trials. I am currently exploring the use of large language models and foundation models in clinical trial design, conduct and analysis.

I am currently a postdoctoral research fellow in Biostatistics, under the guidance of Dr. Qi Long, in the Department of Biostatistics, Epidemiology and Informatics, University of Pennsylvania.

Previously I received my PhD in Biostatistics (supervised by Dr. Robert Platt and Dr. Archer Yi Yang) and Master's degree in Epidemiology (supervised by Dr. Samy Suissa and Dr. Laurent Azoulay), from McGill University, Montréal, Canada. Here is my CV (PDF).
